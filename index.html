Ratan Tata

1937 — 2024

A humble leader, visionary industrialist, and a philanthropist who served India and the world.

About Ratan Tata

Ratan Naval Tata was an Indian industrialist and philanthropist best known for leading the Tata Group and for his lifelong commitment to ethical business and public service. Born on 28 December 1937 in Bombay (now Mumbai), he guided the Tata Group through a period of major expansion and globalisation. He was widely admired for his humility, integrity, and focus on nation-building. He passed away on 9 October 2024, leaving an enduring legacy in industry, innovation and philanthropy.

Early life & family

Ratan Tata was born into the Tata family — a family with a long history of industrial and philanthropic activity in India. His grandfather was Naval Tata and his great-grandfather was Jamsetji Tata, the founder of the Tata Group. Ratan Tata grew up with strong family values and an emphasis on public service.

Education

Ratan Tata attended Campion School in Mumbai and Cathedral & John Connon School. He later studied at Cornell University, USA, where he earned a degree in architecture and structural engineering. Following Cornell, he completed the Advanced Management Program (AMP) at Harvard Business School.

Career & leadership

Ratan Tata began his career with Tata Steel as a trainee and gradually took on more senior roles across the group. He became Chairman of Tata Sons in 1991. During his leadership the Tata Group:

  • Expanded internationally and acquired global brands (for example, Tetley, Jaguar Land Rover).
  • Invested in technology and services — bolstering Tata Consultancy Services (TCS) into a global IT leader.
  • Encouraged innovation and affordable products (such as the Tata Nano project).
  • Prioritised ethical business practices and corporate social responsibility across the group.

Philanthropy

Ratan Tata was deeply involved with Tata Trusts, which support education, healthcare, rural livelihoods and disaster relief across India. He personally supported numerous charitable initiatives, startups, and social enterprises. His philanthropic approach combined long-term institutional funding with a focus on measurable social impact.

Awards & honours

Over his lifetime, Ratan Tata received many honours and awards recognising his contribution to industry and society, including civilian honours and international recognitions. Beyond awards, he was venerated for his character, ethical leadership and willingness to mentor entrepreneurs.

Legacy

Ratan Tata's legacy is visible in the Tata Group's global presence, in Tata Trusts' social work, and in the many leaders and entrepreneurs he inspired. He combined business growth with social purpose and set a standard for responsible leadership in India and abroad.

Notable quote

"I don't believe in taking right decisions. I take decisions and then make them right." — Ratan Tata

Timeline of Major Events:

  • 1937: Born in Bombay (now Mumbai), India (28 December).
  • 1962: Joined Tata Steel as a trainee and worked in multiple Tata companies.
  • 1991: Became Chairman of Tata Sons and began driving modernisation and international expansion.
  • 2000s: Oversaw acquisitions and global expansion (including key investments and partnering).
  • 2008: Launched the Tata Nano project — an ambitious attempt to provide affordable personal mobility.
  • 2012: Stepped down as Chairman of Tata Sons and became Chairman Emeritus; continued to advise and support the Group.
  • 2024: Passed away on 9 October 2024, mourned across industries and the nation.
